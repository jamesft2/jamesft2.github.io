James Tallman

I am a third year PhD Student at the University of Illinois Urbana Champaign.

I work with Prof. Antonia Statt (Statt Lab) using computational methods (mainly molecular dynamics simulations) to understand complex polymeric and biological systems. Our research focuses on the interactions between polymers and phospholipids, and understanding how fundemental physical models can describe complex systems.

Journal Articles & Publications

Graduate Degree

  1. Simulating Curved Lipid Membranes Using Anchored Frozen Patches
    JF Tallman, A Statt, The Journal of Physical Chemistry B, 2025 We present a method for simulating curved lipid membranes by anchoring frozen patches of lipids to create arbitrary curvature. This allows us to simulate out-of-equilibrium ensembles with imposed membrane curvature.
  2. Lipid Membrane Leaflets Unzip upon Hybridization with Polymer-Rich Nanodomains
    JF Tallman, N Kambar, C Leal, A Statt, Macromolecules 57 (24), 11688-11696, 2024 We use molecular dynamics simulations to predict that large hydrophobic polymers can unzip lipid bilayers, which has implications for drug delivery and the design of hybrid lipid-polymer materials. This result is validated through experimental imaging.
